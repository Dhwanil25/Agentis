The problem

One prompt.
One response.
Not enough.

You give an AI a real task. It returns a wall of code. Maybe it works. You have no idea why it made the choices it made. There's no plan, no structure, no thinking visible.

Real work has phases. Planning before building. Reviewing before shipping. Compressing everything into one prompt loses all of that depth.

The insight

Each phase,

The Planner breaks the task apart. The Architect designs the structure. The Coder writes it. The Reviewer catches what was missed. Each skill sees all previous output — the chain compounds.
